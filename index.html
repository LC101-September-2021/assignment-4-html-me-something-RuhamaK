Changi Airport

I got my first glimpse of Singapore from the air as my plane landed at Changi international airport. This is one of the largest airports in Asia is a destination in itself. I was able to see a glimpse of what Singapore had to offer even before I stepped into the city. Changi had a wide variety of shopping & dining options along with myriad attractions for every age group & interest. It would take longer than a day to explore everything Changi had to offer.

Things to see

The island city-state of Singapore is only 719.7 kms in size. However jampacked into this tiny island is a diverse landscape of the modern and the ancient, sleek skyscrapers next to historical buildings. A bustling city that never sleeps with pockets of lush greenery & sleepy neighborhoods where time seems to have stopped. Some of the attractions that I was able to visit were: The Merlion: Singapore's national monument, which has a lion's head & a fish's body. Gardens by the Bay: A nature park housing a diverse collection of plants frome every continent. The 18 avatar like supertrees were one of the highlights of this park. Chinatown & Little India, Sentosa Resort. Other popular tourist attractions:Singapore Zoo, Singapore Botanic gardens, Jurong Bird park.

What to eat

Singapore is a food lovers paradise with every type of cuisine imaginable, from fine dining to street food. The variety of food available is mind boggling & will take more than a short vacation to experience. Singaporean cuisine is a mix of mainly Chinese, Indian & Malay flavors. Some of the local dishes that I enjoyed were: Chilli crab: Singapore's national dish. Laksa: A rich and spicy noodle soup. Satay: Skewered, grilled meat served with peanut sauce. Kaya toast: Coconut jam on toast

Where to shop

Singapore is a shopaholic's dream come true & you can find everything from high street fashion, luxury goods, the latest gadgets to pocket friendly bargain buys. It is easy to make a bunch of impulse buys but if you set a definite budget you can come out without making a huge dent in your wallet. Orchard road with its plethora of malls & shopping centres is a good place to get your shopping fix.

Interesting facts about Singapore

Some interesting facts about Singapore,

  • Singapore’s national anthem is in micro-text on the back of their $1,000 note.
  • Singapore is one of only three surviving city-states in the world. The other two are Monaco and the Vatican City.
  • Singapore is among the 20 smallest countries in the world.
  • Singapore’s Bukit Timah Nature Reserve holds more species of trees than the entire North American continent.
  • The national language of Singapore is Malay. The four official languages of the country are English, Chinese, Tamil, and Malay.
